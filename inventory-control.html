The business challenge

Why the current operating model does not scale.

The problem is rarely a lack of effort. It is the gap between systems, ownership, policy and daily execution.

01

Stock data lacks operational context

ERP quantities do not always explain ownership, condition, usage, reservation or pending movement.

02

Teams react too late

Shortages and excess inventory are often discovered after service, production or cash has already been affected.

03

Controls are separate from movement

Approvals, evidence, serial or batch data and compliance checks are often recorded outside the stock transaction.

Connect inventory decisions to execution and evidence.

SELYVO provides the workflow and control layer around stock data—turning replenishment, transfer, issue, count, quarantine and disposal into visible, governed work.

Exception-led inventory controlPrioritise shortages, excess, ageing and count differences by operational and financial impact.
Controlled movementsApply ownership, approval, evidence and segregation rules to stock transactions.
Supplier-connected replenishmentLink availability risk to supplier lead time, acknowledgement and performance.
Compliance by stock itemAttach required certificates, batch, serial, location and handling evidence.
How SELYVO solves it

A controlled operating flow from signal to outcome.

Every step has a purpose, an owner, the right information and a visible result.

STEP 01

Detect the need

A threshold, forecast, request or count identifies an inventory action.

STEP 02

Assess the impact

SELYVO checks availability, demand, criticality, value and supplier options.

STEP 03

Choose the response

Replenish, transfer, reserve, substitute, count, quarantine or dispose.

STEP 04

Approve and execute

Relevant control and evidence requirements are applied automatically.

STEP 05

Confirm movement

Receipt, issue or transfer is recorded with owner, location and supporting data.

STEP 06

Monitor exceptions

Management sees unresolved shortages, ageing, variances and supplier causes.

Daily operation
