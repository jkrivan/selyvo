The business challenge

Why the current operating model does not scale.

The problem is rarely a lack of effort. It is the gap between systems, ownership, policy and daily execution.

01

Demand appears too late

Finance often sees spend only when an invoice arrives, leaving little room to challenge, redirect or budget accurately.

02

Approvals are inconsistent

Email, spreadsheets and local practices create gaps in authority, evidence and policy compliance.

03

Supplier and invoice data are fragmented

Procurement, AP and business teams work from different records, slowing decisions and creating duplicate effort.

Move financial control to the point of decision.

SELYVO creates a single operating flow from request to payment. Rules, data and evidence travel with the transaction, while automation handles routine validation and routing.

Guided demand intakeUsers submit a structured request and are directed to catalogues, contracts, sourcing or exception routes.
Commitment visibilityApproved requisitions and purchase orders create an early view of financial exposure.
Three-way controlOrders, receipts and invoices are matched, with exceptions assigned to a named owner.
Policy built into workApproval limits, preferred suppliers and evidence requirements are applied automatically.
How SELYVO solves it

A controlled operating flow from signal to outcome.

Every step has a purpose, an owner, the right information and a visible result.

STEP 01

Capture demand

A user raises a request with business need, accounting, delivery and supporting evidence.

STEP 02

Validate automatically

SELYVO checks policy, supplier status, budget, duplicate demand and required fields.

STEP 03

Route the decision

Approvals follow value, category, entity, risk and delegation-of-authority rules.

STEP 04

Source or order

The request moves to sourcing, contract call-off, catalogue or direct purchase order.

STEP 05

Confirm delivery

Goods or services are received with evidence and responsibility recorded.

STEP 06

Match and pay

Invoices are matched; exceptions are routed before approved data reaches the ERP or payment process.
